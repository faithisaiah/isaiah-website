Acceleration

Acceleration
Rate of Change of Velocity with Time.
Acceleration is the Second Deriviation of Displacement.
Measured in Metre per second squared m/s2.
Acceleration = Average Velocity = V2 - V1
                     Total Average time      t2 - t1
where,
V2 = Final Velocity
V1 = Initial Velocity
t2 = Final Time
t1 = Initial Time
Instantenous Acceleration
Rate of Average Velocity and Total Average Time,
as Total Average Time Approaches Zero.
Acceleration = Lim = Average Velocity = V2 - V1
                       At-->0      Average Time       t2 - t1
where,
V2 = Final Velocity
V1 = Initial Velocity
t2 = Final Time
t1 = Initial Time
At = Average Time
Lim = Limit as Average Velocity Time Approaches Zero.
