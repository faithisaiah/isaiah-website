Mass And Weight

Mass
Quantity of Matter(has mass and Occupies space)
in a body.

Exact Value or Quantity(amount,size) of a Substance.
Mass is a Scalar Quantity.
Measured kilogram (kg) as its Unit.

Weight
Magnitude of Force Acting on a Body or an Object

Weight is a Veactor Quantity,Denoted in W(weber).
Measured in Newton (N).



Why Weight is a Vector Quantity,
1. Shape of the Earth, which is Not Spherical(not Round).
2. Distance of the Body or Object from the Centre of the Earth.
3. Magnitude(size) of Force or Weight of Force of Attraction
of the Body,to the Centre of the Earth.

Acceleration Due to Gravity

Acceleration due to Gravity is Weight(force of Gravity).
Acceleration due to Gravity is MORE at the Pole, and LESS at the Equator.

Relationship Between MASS and WEIGHT

W = m x g
W = m(kg) x g
W = Mg

W = weight

g = Gravity force.

3. What Makes a Body or an Object to Move,

FRICTION

Friction
Oppossing Force Acting on Two Surfaces,
Sliding Over each other in Relative Motion.


Limitng Friction.
Forces that Acts when Two Surfaces are in Contact.


Body Rolled on Smooth surface
It Moves for a Longer Time Before It Stops,
or Comes to Rest.


Body Rolled on Rough surface
It Moves for a Shorter Time Before It Stops,
or Comes to Rest.

Friction is a Vector Quantity.

Three States of Friction
i. Solid State.
ii. Liquid State.
iii. Gases or Gaseous State.

When a Weight(mass) is Added to a Body or an Object,
The Point at Which the New Weight(mass) added Makes
the Body or Object to Move or Start Moving is known as
LIMITING FRICTION.

Advantages of Friction,
i. Makes Slippery Surfaces Rough.
ii. Brakes of Motors.
iii. Rough Surfaces Increases Friction.
iv. Friction causes Man's Possibility to walk
freely Balanced.
Disadvantages of Friction,
i. Opposses or Reduces Motion.
ii. Causes Wears and Tears in Machines.
iii. Reduces machines Efficiency
iv. Causes heating of Engines.

Applications Of Friction,

Brakes Of Motors:
Smooth surfaces takes Longer time before
Coming to Rest (SLIDES).
While,
Rough Surfaces take a Shorter time before
Coming to Rest (STOPS).
Sands:
Smooth or Slippery Surfaces are Made Rough,
by Pouring Sand on the Surface.
Belt and Pulley:
Belt and Pulley Frictional Force Causes Rotation
of Bodies or Parts of Machines
Man's Feet and Ground Surface:
Friction Between the Feet Surface and Ground Surface,
Causes Man's Possibility to Walk without Falling.

Methods Of Reducing Friction.

Streamlined
Object or Body Shaped(fish shape) and Ensure that,
Small Surface is Lining In Head-On Contact(like force)
With Fluid(Air,Water).
Lubrication
Viscous Oil or Liquid Applied at two Surfaces,
Sliding Together in Contact.
Polish
Polishing two Surfaces in Contact,
Example: bearings of watches
Minimising interlocking(tie up Together) and
Projection(object represented on a flat Surface).
Banking
Blocks built by the Roadsides
Ball Bearing
Shafts are Fixed to Ball Bearing to Reduce Friction.
Example: Wheels of Tyres,Axle.
Pencils and Polystyrene
Pencils:Written details of an Arrangement,
that can be changed later.
Polystyrene:Light Soft Plastic,used for Making
Containers that Prevents Heat Loss.
